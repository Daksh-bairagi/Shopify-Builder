# Contribution Note

This project was built by a solo participant.

I spent the first 2 days on product thinking: clarifying the problem, narrowing the scope, defining the evaluation model, and deciding what would make the product and demo credible. After that, I created an 8-day engineering plan to build the system in phases while intentionally leaving time for refinement, verification, testing, and polish before submission.

Once the 8-day engineering plan was completed on schedule, I focused on tightening the product experience, improving error handling, and making the critical implementation decisions needed to make the system more reliable and presentation-ready.

A major share of the implementation effort went into making the core system genuinely work end to end: the 19-check audit framework, the fix-planning and fix-execution flow, and the supporting verification/rollback behavior. A lot of time also went into the report-building, API/state handling, and test coverage needed to make the output feel trustworthy rather than decorative.

For perception-style surfaces, the goal was to build meaningful product evidence rather than over-polish every edge. Those features are not refined to their absolute endpoint, but they are strong enough to clearly show merchants where representation gaps exist and how the system interprets those weaknesses.
